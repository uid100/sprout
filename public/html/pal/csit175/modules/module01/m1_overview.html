Me

My career began in research. In that environment, I was primarily responsible for finding creative solutions for real world data collection and analysis in challenging environments. Sometimes it meant writing software for instrument control and data processing on a small ship rolling in heavy seas. Sometimes I was building cabinets, designing and creating electrical and plumbing solutions for a mobile shipboard lab facility. Sometimes it was negotiating with shipping agents and import/export documentation in foreign ports. Always, it involved a changing set of requirements, analysis, design, and implementation.

Moving into the private sector, I ran IT departments for large banking operations, again looking for creative solutions to apply emerging technologies.

Then, for many more years, I worked as a systems engineer and later project and program manager for large government contractors.

I still work part-time as a consultant, both as program manager and systems software engineer. I began teaching and for the last few years I've had the pleasure of teaching a variety of computer science topics at a number of different colleges and universities.

How is this going to work?

Classroom Meetings

We will meet in class each week according to the course schedule. Please by on time and treat our classroom as the professional environment that it is. See syllabus for more.

Assignments and quizzes are due each week. See the schedule for dates. The materials for each module will be available by the start of each week, and I will try and make them available sooner. You should read the chapter and start the exercises for each module before class. I will be going over the materials, walking through examples, and answering questions, but you will get more out of the class if it is not your first time seeing the material.

Complete your assignments and get them submitted on time. It is my goal to get your projects graded before the class meeting the next week so that I can answer questions about them. I can't guarantee that late assignments will be graded. Late and missing assignments are not fair to me, to you, or to other students who are turning them in on time. See the syllabus for more details about my policy on late work.

This course is designed to be very much a hands-on experience. Try and search for solutions when you run into challenges and try to apply them. There will be some reading and some videos to explain finer points of solutions development, and more importantly there will be opportunities to design and create.

Notes and Resources

The goal of each module is to make available to you a variety of learning resources and give you an organized list of materials to help you master each chapter. Everybody learns differently so I'm going to leave it mostly up to you to choose and use the materials that help you learn best.

The Power Point slides are from the publisher and correspond to the textbook. Many people find these slides helpful as a study guide for the chapter and for taking notes.

There may be helpful links on the #resources channel on Discord also, as well as friendly people to help answer questions. See the Discord link on the front page and the syllabus to join my server and online class discussions.

Practice, practice, practice.

If this is your first time taking any kind of programming course, don't panic - you're in the right place! It may not seem like it at times but really, you are. Learning a programming language is much like learning a foreign language or learning to play a musical instrument. The key and secret is practice! You have to immerse yourself and get involved hands-on. Simply trying to skim the chapter or a quick look at the sample code isn't going to do you a whole lot of good.

Coding is fun.

In almost every lab assignment, there will be at least one guided programming exercise designed to prepare you for completing the remaining lab exercises.

We will work on these together in class but you are encouraged to work them on your own ahead of time and bring your questions and share your experience.
